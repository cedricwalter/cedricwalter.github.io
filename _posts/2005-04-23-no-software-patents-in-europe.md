The European commission has just passed its directive on software patents, violating democratic rules and procedures to
the sole benefit of big non-European corporation and Ireland and to the detriment of small and medium sized businesses (
which comprise 99% of the European software industry) and free software.

The European parliament will now be taking the last stand against software patents in a voting for which an absolute
majority is needed. Such a majority is hard to come by in a parliament with a low attendance level.

But not all is lost yet as long as **you** decide it is time to make a difference and take action. This is our last
opportunity to fend off software patents worldwide, there will be no second chance for the foreseeable future.

[Signing petitions](http://petition.eurolinux.org/) will not suffice. Contact your local EU representatives and educate
them why [software patents are a bad idea in the first place](http://lpf.ai.mit.edu/Patents/industry-at-risk.html) and
why they **must** attend that parliament session to vote against them. Make it clear that they need to stop
the [machinations of the EU council](http://wiki.ffii.org/LtrFfiiCons050308En) and reaffirm the power of the EU
parliament, the only democratically elected EU institution. For in-depth information and starting points to get active
visit the [software patent page of the FFII (Foundation for a Free Information Infrastructure)](http://swpat.ffii.org/)
and [NoSoftwarePatents.com](http://nosoftwarepatents.com/).

it time to say No to software patents or #Linux, Videolan, Mplayer, Gimp, OpenOffice, Firefox and all GPL tool will
disapear!!!!!
