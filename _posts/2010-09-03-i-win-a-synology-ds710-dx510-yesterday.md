I did win a Synology DS710+ &amp; DX510 by filling out a survey, after having read an official tweet of Synology Inc.(<http://twitter.com/Synology>) about the contest 3 weeks ago. The funny part is that I bought a [Synology](http://www.synology.com) DS1010+ (704CHF/692$) and 4TB of disk (280CHF/275$) at the same time…

On the picture above, this is my current small datacenter 🙂

- DS 408 has four disks of 1TB in RAID5, so a volume of 2.7TB since 2008
- DS1010+ has two disks of 2 TB in RAID1 so a volume of 1.8TB since 2008
- I have others disks 3.5TB not represented in my custom NAS that will soon be retired…

The DS 510 will perfectly complete my existing DS1010+, the DS710+ will be assigned to some other tasks. Synology is really developing high-performance, reliable, versatile, and environmentally-friendly Network Attached Storage products. I did never had any problems, and all software updates were working as expected. They are by far the best NAS on the market with a lot of software functionalities thanks to a #Linux kernel.

## Synology DX510

Expansion Unit for Increasing Capacity of the [Synology](http://www.synology.com) DiskStation DS710+ or DS1010+. When the storage capacity of the [Synology](http://www.synology.com) DS710+/DS1010+ is nearing its limits, it can easily be expanded by the [Synology](http://www.synology.com) DX510. The [Synology](http://www.synology.com) DX510 securely connects to the [Synology](http://www.synology.com) DS710+/DS1010+ via an eSATA cable of specially-designed connectors on both ends to ensure maximum throughput. The [Synology](http://www.synology.com) DX510 can directly expand the existing storage on the [Synology](http://www.synology.com) DS710+ to a maximum 14 TB volume.
