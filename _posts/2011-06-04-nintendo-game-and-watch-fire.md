# *[![Nintendo_Game_and_Watch_Fire_00](/assets/images/2011/06/Nintendo_Game_and_Watch_Fire_00_thumb.jpg "Nintendo_Game_and_Watch_Fire_00")](/assets/images/2011/06/Nintendo_Game_and_Watch_Fire_00.jpg)***

> ***Fire*** was the fourth Game &amp; Watch game to be released. It was made by Nintendo Research &amp; Development 1 and released on July 31, 1980 (product number RC-04 under the Silver series). It was one of the most popular games in the Game &amp; Watch series. Due to Fire’s popularity, another version was released on December 4, 1981 (product number FR-27, shown on the photo). That version was part of the Wide Screen series.
> 
> In the Silver Screen version, people are falling from a burning building. The player controls two firemen who carry a trampoline. They have to bounce all the people to a waiting ambulance. When they get a person in the ambulance the player scores 1 point. But if a person hits the ground, the player loses a life. There are three lives. If the player loses them all they lose the game. In the Wide Screen version, gameplay is very similar except there are a few changes. The biggest change of all is the fact that the player only needs to bounce a person to score a point instead of having to get them in the ambulance. Another change is that the Wide Screen version was more colorful and had better graphics than the Silver Series version. \[From [WikiPedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Game_%26_Watch_games#Fire)\]
