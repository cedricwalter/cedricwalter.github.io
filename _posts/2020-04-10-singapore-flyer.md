The [Singapore](https://waltercedric.com/tag/singapore/) Flyer is a giant observation wheel at [Downtown Core](https://www.openstreetmap.org/search?query=Singapore%20Flyer#map=17/1.28909/103.86262), [Singapore](https://waltercedric.com/tag/singapore/). It opened in 2008, with construction having taken about 2½ years. It carried its first paying passengers on 11 February, had a soft opening to the public on 1 March, and was then officially opened on 15 April. It has 28 air-conditioned capsules, each able to accommodate 28 passengers, and incorporates a three-story terminal building.

<iframe class="uagb-google-map__iframe" src="https://www.google.com/maps/embed/v1/place?key=AIzaSyAsd_d46higiozY-zNqtr7zdA81Soswje4&q=singapore%20flyer&zoom=14" style="height:300px"></iframe>The Flyer has an overall height of 165 metres (541 ft) and was the world’s tallest Ferris wheel until the 167.6 m (550 ft) High Roller, which is 2.6 m (9 ft) taller than the Flyer, opened on the Las Vegas Strip in Nevada, US, on 31 March 2014.

Visited 16 November 2018
