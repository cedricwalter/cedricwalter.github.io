| ![](/assets/images/2006/11/java.eclipse.5th.birthday.jpg) | ## Community Bulletin  Eclipse is turning 5! It was November 7, 2001 when IBM first released #Eclipse as an open source project. To celebrate, a number of activities are being organized for the #Eclipse community.  **Birthday Card**  An electronic [birthday card](http://birthday.eclipse.org/images/map.php?individual) has been setup to allow individuals in the the #Eclipse community to show their support for #Eclipse. Everyone in the #Eclipse community is encouraged to [sign the #Eclipse birthday card](http://birthday.eclipse.org/info.php?individual).  Individuals signing the card before December 1, 2006 will qualify for a random draw to win one of 20 #Eclipse sweaters.  **Product Hall of Fame**   Over the last five years, the #Eclipse community has built hundreds, if not thousands, of #Eclipse based products. Therefore, an [Eclipse Product Wall of Fame](http://birthday.eclipse.org/images/map.php?builder) has been setup to showcase these products. Organizations and individuals that have created #Eclipse based products and plug-ins are invited to [add their solution to the Product Wall of Fame](http://birthday.eclipse.org/info.php?builder).  **Eclipse Birthday Parties**   There are over 25 local #Eclipse birthday parties organized in cities around the world to celebrate this event. Locations, dates, and RSVP links can be found [here.](http://www.eclipse.org/community/eclipsebirthday5/birthdayparties.php)  And here [is my submission](http://birthday.eclipse.org/images/usermessage.php?vid=1803) |
