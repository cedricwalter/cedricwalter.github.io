I cant personally blog anymore while being logged in a web editor (being either in the frontend or the backend of #Joomla). I like to prepare my articles offline. Unfortunately XML-RPC plugins have been removed out of the core of [Joomla 1.7](http://www.joomla.org/) and are not maintained anymore (an error for me). And here is how to bring back this nice feature working in [Joomla 1.7](http://www.joomla.org/)

Install this extension [Blogging XMLRPC for #Joomla!](http://extensions.joomla.org/extensions/news-production/blog/blog-integratios/16655) ([direct link to download)](http://www.joomler.net/download/agreement/81-pkg-xmlrpc-1-7-1.html?tmpl=component) and then [follow the tutorial](http://www.joomler.net/documents/141-xmlrpc-for-joomla/952-why-does-livewriter-ask-the-blog-type.html).

In short, the tutorial explains you to publish these 2 extensions plugins

5. XMLRPC – #Joomla
6. System – RSD
You can now start your favorite XML-RPC client, I personally use

8. [Microsoft Windows Live Writer](http://explore.live.com/windows-live-writer), 
9. Or Get [ScribeFire](http://www.scribefire.com/) for [Firefox](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/1730) – [Chrome](https://chrome.google.com/extensions/detail/elkkomimknapgodalnkjeddkjnjkfmfp) – [Safari](http://www.scribefire.com/2010/06/07/scribefire-and-safari-sitting-in-a-tree/) – [Opera](https://addons.opera.com/addons/extensions/details/scribefire/)
 If you use Windows Live writer and add your blog, it will AutoDetect all settings.
