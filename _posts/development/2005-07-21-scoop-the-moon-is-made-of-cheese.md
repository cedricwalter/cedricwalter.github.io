| ![](/assets/images/2005/07/moonphoto.jpg) | Yes the moon is made of cheese….. 🙂   In honor of the first manned Moon landing, which took place on July 20, 1969, Google added some NASA imagery to the [Google Maps](http://maps.google.com/) interface and present [Google map moon.](http://moon.google.com/)So Google localizing  offering is now made of:   - [Google map moon](http://moon.google.com/), this is where You can see with your own eyes that the moon is made of cheese (just use the zoom function to see the maximum of details). <font size="1" style="font-style: italic;">" Google Moon only has as much data as NASA was able to give us, so there are limitations (for now) on how close to the surface we can zoom." - [Google earth](http://earth.google.com/): "<font size="1" style="font-style: italic;">Google Earth puts a planet’s worth of imagery and other geographic information right on your desktop. View exotic locales like Maui and Paris as well as points of interest such as local restaurants, hospitals, schools, and more." - <font size="2" style="font-style: italic;">[Google maps](http://maps.google.com/): to find a location, find a business (shops, restaurants others), or just go from one point to the other. Only USA is currently supported, but Europe is planned. |
