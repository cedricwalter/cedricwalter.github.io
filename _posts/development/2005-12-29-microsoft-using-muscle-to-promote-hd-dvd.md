Now the EE Times is reporting that the company may attempt to leverage its OS dominance to push HD DVD penetration in the PC market.*  *Several industry sources last week told EE Times that Microsoft is muscling into the optical-disk fray by leveraging its operating-system clout to bundle HD-DVD within Vista, the company’s next-generation OS. There is also talk that the software giant may be planning to offer cash incentives â&#128;&#148; in the form "coupons" â&#128;&#148; to system vendors or retailers if they agree to support HD-DVD. The most common theory is that Microsoft is ultimately doing anything in its power to put the hurt on Sony’s PlayStation 3, which will come equipped with a Blu-ray drive. The theory says that Microsoft is worried that the PS3 will dominate gaming once again, partially on account of the device’s potential dual-role in the household: gaming console and Blu-ray player.
