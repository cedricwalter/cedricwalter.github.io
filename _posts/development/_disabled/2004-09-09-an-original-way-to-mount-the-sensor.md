| [![correct cooling fan](/assets/images/2004/09/Field_Day_2004_005-.jpg)](/assets/images/2004/09/Field_Day_2004_005.jpg)    [![global view](/assets/images/2004/09/Field_Day_2004_007-.jpg)](/assets/images/2004/09/Field_Day_2004_007.jpg)    [![sensor mounted](/assets/images/2004/09/Field_Day_2004_010-.jpg)](/assets/images/2004/09/Field_Day_2004_010.jpg)    [![result](/assets/images/2004/09/Field_Day_2004_013-.jpg)](/assets/images/2004/09/Field_Day_2004_013.jpg) | “*Finding out that the GV-1 Brackets were not meant to be used with a Caliber 30’s clutch hub being directly in the way, I decided to get creative.        This worked out perfect, and is probably better that the normal installation method. I used a cooling fan from Correct, (item 416-141) and used the rim around the top to install the magnet. I then mounted the sensor on the TOP of the fan facing down and drilled a hole and shaped it to fit the sensor.         The sensor now sits above the fan facing down, and is receiving at a 97% (which is very good). It also is mounted to the frame instead of a long bracket, and doesn’t move around. This method also makes for a much cleaner look, as there is no brackets used to mount.*“        All credits to [sgunn911](http://www.runryder.com/member.htm?action=getinfo&userid=16941), [his gallery at runryder](http://www.runryder.com/gallery/16941/) and [website](http://www.custom-rc.com/index.html) [CUSTOM-RC.COM](http://www.custom-rc.com/) |
