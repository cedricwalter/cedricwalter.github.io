# Picture

The default settings (out of the box “dynamic” is pre set) make any show just unwatchable: everything look too hell, colors are denatured. As always tune to your liking, by popping up some Blu-Ray you really like until you get the outstanding picture that this TV really deserve. Don’t get me wrong, it is not a huge task (2-3 minutes in my case) and you’ll really feel the difference!

Samsung has solved the light coming from the corners (Edge lightning) that was hitting previous model line of 2009. I found some lighting uniformity issues while watching a blu-ray ([Surf’s Up](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0423294/) has some white text on a black background) something known as **backlight Clouding Issue**: this should not appear in this model

I got two Samsung SSG-2200AR Active Shutter Glasses in the package.

To start them all you have to do is just to push the small power button , glasses sync in no time and work nearly immediately. They turn off to save battery in the following case

- TV content is no more in 3D
- You switch the TV off
- You press the power button for a few seconds

NOTE: These glasses are working like sunglasses and remove some brightness (more than 40% i would say) when viewing 3D content.

# Media Center

You can connect a USB external hard drive and play music, photo’s and movies directly from the HDD.

# Internet TV

Internet@TV lets you enjoy more with Internet TV, as long as you connect the television with an Ethernet cable or an optional WIFI dongle (+$80). You can get a lot of small applet to run, just to name a few:

- eBay, real-time updates, place bids, monitor your list of watched items
- Twitter, the famous micro blogging
- Yahoo! News
- Yahoo! Weather
- Flickr
- YouTube
- Google MAPS

Sound great? yes but

Widgets are quite slow to startup, the remote control is VERY limited to enter text: a Bluetooth keyboard would be welcomed but is not supported…try to enter an address in Google Maps and you will understand what I mean.
