[See the search globe yourself,](http://data-arts.appspot.com/globe-search) best viewed in google chrome ![Smile](/assets/images/2011/02/wlEmoticon-smile.png)

> Developed and designed by the Google Data Arts Team using WebGL, the [backend](http://googlecode.blogspot.com/2011/05/visualizing-geographic-data-with-webgl.html) of the technology uses your computer’s hardware to generate fast, 3D graphics. Google says that in order to use Search Globe you need a WebGL-enabled browser (like Google Chrome), to see the Globe.

and they open source the project so developers can build their own globe

> The **WebGL Globe** is an open platform for visualizing geographic information in WebGL enabled browsers. It supports data in JSON format, a sample of which you can find [here](https://github.com/dataarts/dat.globe/raw/master/globe/population909500.json). dat.globe makes heavy use of the [Three.js](https://github.com/mrdoob/three.js/) library, and is still in early open development.

Get the code at <https://github.com/dataarts/dat.globe>
