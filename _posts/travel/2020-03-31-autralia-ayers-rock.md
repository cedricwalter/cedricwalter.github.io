Uluru, or Ayers Rock, is a massive sandstone monolith in the heart of the Northern Territory’s arid “Red Centre”. The
nearest large town is Alice Springs, 450km away. Uluru is sacred to indigenous Australians and is thought to have
started forming around 550 million years ago. It’s within Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park, which also includes the 36
red-rock domes of the Kata Tjuta (colloquially “The Olgas”) formation.
