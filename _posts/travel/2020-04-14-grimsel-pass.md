The Grimselpass is a high mountain pass in the Swiss Alps. It connects the canton of Bern with the canton of Valais and
is one of the most important passes in Switzerland. The pass road is only open from June to October and is often closed
due to snow.

The Grimselpass is located in the Bernese Oberland region of Switzerland and is one of the most popular tourist
destinations in the country. Every year, thousands of people come to experience the stunning scenery and to enjoy the
many activities on offer.

There are several ways to reach the Grimselpass, but the most popular is by car. The pass road is well-maintained and
offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. If you are driving, be sure to take your time and enjoy the journey.

Once you reach the top of the pass, there are plenty of things to do. There are several hiking trails that lead off into
the mountains, so you can explore at your own pace. If you’re feeling adventurous, you can even try your hand at
climbing some of the peaks.

If you’d prefer to take it easy, there are also plenty of places to relax and take in the views. There are several
restaurants and cafes at the top of the pass, so you can refuel after a day of exploring.

No matter how you choose to spend your time at Grimselpass, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience.

The Grimsel Pass (German: *Grimselpass*; French: *Col du Grimsel*; Italian: *Passo del Grimsel*) is a mountain pass in
Switzerland, crossing the Bernese Alps at an elevation of 2,164 metres (7,100 ft). The pass connects the Haslital, the
upper valley of the river Aare, with the upper valley of the Rhône. In so doing, and as the Aare is a tributary of the
Rhine, the pass crosses the continental divide between the North Sea and the Mediterranean Sea.

A paved road follows the pass, running 38 kilometres (24 mi) from Gletsch to Meiringen. The road is normally closed
between October and May, due to the high snowfall on the pass. As it is the only direct road pass between the cantons of
Bern and Valais across the Bernese Alps, attempts are made to keep the road open as long as possible with snow ploughs.
A PostBus Switzerland service uses the pass several times a day, connecting Meiringen and
Oberwald. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grimsel_Pass)
