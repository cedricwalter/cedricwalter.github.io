The Sustenpass is a high mountain pass in the Swiss Alps. It connects the canton of Uri with the canton of Bern and is
one of the most important passes in Switzerland. The pass road is only open from June to October and is often closed due
to snowfall.

The Sustenpass is located in the central Alps, between the Gotthard massif and the Bernese Oberland. It connects the
Reuss valley in Uri with the Aare valley in Bern. The pass road is about 24 km long and reaches a height of 2,224 m
above sea level.

The Sustenpass was first used by Roman troops in order to cross the Alps. In medieval times, it was an important trade
route between Italy and northern Europe. Today, it is a popular tourist destination and a starting point for many hikes
and mountain bike tours.

The name Susten comes from the Latin word sustinere, which means "to support". This is probably a reference to the fact
that the pass road was built on wooden supports in order to prevent it from being washed away by avalanches.

The Sustenpass is known for its beautiful scenery and its many hairpin bends. It is also one of the most challenging
roads in Switzerland, with a steep gradient of up to 12%.

If you are planning to drive over the Sustenpass, make sure to check the weather forecast in advance. Due to its high
altitude, the pass road is often closed due to snowfall or avalanches.

Susten Pass (German: *Sustenpass*) is a mountain pass in the Swiss Alps. The pass road, built from 1938–1945, connects
Innertkirchen in the canton of Bern with Wassen in the canton of Uri. A 300-metre long tunnel crosses the pass at 2,224
metres.

The pass is popular with tourists, by car, motorbike or bicycles especially for the views of the Stein Glacier on the south side. 
There are numerous great views and a number of places to stop for photos. The views are stunning.
